# Accessibility Navigator - Accessibility Audit and Presentation

## Overview
**Objective:**
Evaluate candidates' ability to conduct a theoretical truncated accessibility audit and then present the actionable insights to stakeholders.

---

## What the Manager is Assessing
The **NYS Example Agency** needs to assess accessibility status across **10–15 high-traffic government websites/apps**. This agency has a primary website and several smaller initiative-based websites and helper applications. The goal is to identify systemic issues, prioritize remediation efforts, and align with WCAG 2.2 AA standards. This is a time sensitive project that doesn't have the luxury of conducting a full scale audit of each digital site or application.

## Your Scenario
Imagine that you have been tasked with auditing the most high traffic website in that Example Agencies portfolio. 

---

## Keep in mind!
Digital accessibility is first and always for the benefit of real people. What we're trying to do here is promote equal access to information, resources, services and benefits. Throughout this exercise - and your career in accessibility - remember to consult and keep in focus the lived experiences of real disabled users. 

---

## Exercise Breakdown

### Task 1: Baseline Audit and Evaluation Strategy
**Objective:** Describe how you would approach a baseline audit, focusing on what is important to test and present and how this connects to real users.

#### Requirements:
- **Tools & Methods:**
  - List tools you would use (e.g., axe DevTools, WAVE, Lighthouse, Screen Readers, Platforms, Devices, etc.).
  - Explain where automated tools would be useful and what manual testing should focus on.
  - Indicate where in the process manual review would be feasible and how much time it would take and/or the approach to handling this.

- **Accessibility Evaluation:**
  - Define a sampling strategy for key pages (homepages, search functions, forms).
  - What is useful to test - and what is useful to present - considering the scope and timing?
  - How would you standardize or present this accessibility data in a plain-language focused way?

---

### Task 2: Analysis & Insights 
**Objective:** Conceptually define how you would present a standardized accessibility report card to stakeholders that highlights insights and opportunities for improving accessibility on this website as well as ensuring that a wide range of stakeholders (technical, public relations, program area, executive staff) can understand their digital accessibility status/health/readiness. 

#### Requirements:
- **Trends & Patterns to consider:**
  - How would you prioritize findings based on impact and effort?
  - What would you focus on as the most impactful and what would you leave out?

- **Reporting Tools:**
  - Explain how you would approach reporting this information to the target audiences (presentation, documents, digital report cards, data visualizations, etc.).


### Cheat Codes
- If "Example Agency" is too vague - use a real NYS agency that has a large footprint like NYS Department of Health, Department of Labor, or Office of General Services. 
    - Or any large Federal Government Agency / Foreign Government Service (GOV.uk etc)
- Don't use NY.gov or Governor.ny.gov as examples: we're not agencies! 
- A PowerPoint is fine as a presentational tool, but keep in mind that **presentations are not products**. 
- Ideally, the report card and the data must be public and shareable in easy to access digital formats (hint: a webpage!). You don't have to build the website, but describe what it might contain.
