# Accessibility Navigator Manager - Large-Scale Accessibility Exercise

## Overview
**Objective:**
Evaluate candidates' ability to build a conceptual framework to gather baseline accessibility data across multiple government websites/apps efficiently and present actionable insights to stakeholders.

---

## Scenario
The **NYS Example Agency** needs to assess accessibility status across **10–15 high-traffic government websites/apps**. This agency has a primary website and several smaller initiative-based websites and helper applications. The goal is to identify systemic issues, prioritize remediation efforts, and align with WCAG 2.2 AA standards. This is a time sensitive project that doesn't have the luxury of conducting a full scale audit of each digital site or application.

---

## Exercise Breakdown

### Task 1: Baseline Audit and Evaluation Strategy
**Objective:** Design a scalable approach for collecting accessibility data across multiple sites.

#### Requirements:
- **Tools & Methods:**
  - List tools you would use (e.g., axe DevTools, WAVE, Lighthouse).
  - Explain how you would automate or semi-automate the process.
  - Indicate where in the process manual review would be feasible and how much time it would take and/or the approach to handling this.

- **Accessibility Evaluation Plan:**
  - Define a sampling strategy for key pages (homepages, search functions, forms).
  - How would you handle dynamic content and complex interactivity?
  - How would you standardize or present this accessibility data in a plain-language focused way?

#### Guiding Questions:
1. What is your approach to prioritizing sites and subsites based on accessibility risk?
2. How would you ensure consistency in data collection across different tools?
3. What is useful to test - and what is useful to present - considering the scope and timing?

---

### Task 2: Analysis & Insights 
**Objective:** Conceptually define how you would present a standardized accessibility report card to stakeholders that highlights insights and opportunities for improving accessibility across their portfolio as well as ensuring that a wide range of stakeholders (technical, public relations, program area, executive staff) can understand their digital accessibility status/health/readiness. 

#### Requirements:
- **Trends & Patterns to consider:**
  - How would you prioritize findings based on impact and effort?
  - What would you focus on as the most impactful and what would you leave out?
  - How would you continue to engage with the Agency in the future to re-test or to regularly update the report card data?
  - Identify systemic patterns across sites (e.g., common gaps and failures).

- **Reporting Tools:**
  - Explain how you would approach reporting this information to the target audiences (presentation, documents, digital report cards, data visualizations, etc.).
  - Define thresholds for categorizing issues (e.g., critical vs. minor).


### Cheat Codes
- If "Example Agency" is too vague - use a real NYS agency that has a large footprint like NYS Department of Health, Department of Labor, or Office of General Services. 
    - Or any large Federal Government Agency / Foreign Government Service (GOV.uk etc)
- Don't use NY.gov or Governor.ny.gov as examples: we're not agencies! 
- A PowerPoint is fine as a presentational tool, but keep in mind that **presentations are not products**. The report card and the data must be public and shareable in easy to access digital formats (hint: a webpage!). You don't have to build the website, but describe what it would contain.
